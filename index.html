Effects of lead

What can you do?

Knowing the dangers of lead in your school's water, you can take several actions. Advocate for regular water testing to monitor lead levels and ensure the safety of students and staff. Encourage your school to replace old pipes and fixtures that may contain lead, and promote the use of certified water filters that can remove lead from drinking water.

Where is Lead Found?

Lead is commonly found in a school's water system from sources such as old lead pipes, plumbing fixtures, and lead solder used to join copper pipes. Over time, these materials can corrode and release lead into the water. Fixtures made of brass or chrome-plated brass, as well as service lines connecting buildings to the main water supply, can also contribute to lead contamination.

It is important to regularly test the water for lead levels and replace old pipes and fixtures to ensure safe drinking water.

What is lead?

Lead is a naturally occurring heavy metal that has been used throughout history in a variety of products due to its malleability and resistance to corrosion. It was commonly used in pipes, paint, and solder in plumbing systems. Despite its practical applications, lead is highly toxic, particularly to young children and pregnant women. When lead enters the body, it can accumulate over time and cause serious health problems.

What can lead do to you?

Lead poisoning is extremely detrimental to health, especially in children. It can cause irreversible damage to the brain and nervous system, resulting in developmental delays, learning disabilities, and behavioral problems that can persist into adulthood. Pregnant women exposed to lead may suffer miscarriages or have babies with serious developmental issues.

There is no cure for lead poisoning; once it enters the body, it accumulates over time, leading to long-term health problems such as kidney damage, high blood pressure, and cognitive decline. Preventing exposure to lead, particularly in places like schools, is crucial as the effects are devastating and irreversible
